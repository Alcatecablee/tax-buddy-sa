## VAT (Value-Added Tax) Comprehensive Guide

### Understanding VAT in South Africa

#### What is VAT?

VAT is a consumption tax charged on most goods and services at each stage of production and distribution. The standard rate is **15%** as of 2025/26.

#### VAT System Overview

- **Input VAT**: VAT paid on business purchases
- **Output VAT**: VAT charged on business sales
- **Net VAT**: Output VAT minus Input VAT
- **Refund/Payment**: Depends on net position

### VAT Registration Requirements

#### Mandatory Registration Thresholds

- **R1,000,000**: Taxable supplies in 12-month period
- **Expected Turnover**: Next 12 months projection exceeds R1,000,000
- **Registration Deadline**: 21 days from threshold breach
- **Effective Date**: First day of month following registration

#### Voluntary Registration

- **Minimum Threshold**: R50,000 annual taxable supplies
- **Benefits**:
  - Claim input VAT on business expenses
  - Zero-rate exports for VAT purposes
  - Enhanced business credibility
- **Considerations**: Administrative burden vs benefits

#### VAT Number Format

- **Format**: 4xxxxxxxxx (10 digits starting with 4)
- **Verification**: Use SARS eTax system
- **Display Requirements**: All tax invoices and documentation

### VAT Return Periods & Deadlines

#### Return Frequencies

**Monthly Returns**

- **Requirement**: Annual turnover > R30 million
- **Submission**: 25th of month following VAT period
- **Payment**: Same date as submission deadline

**Bi-Monthly Returns**

- **Requirement**: Annual turnover R1.5 million - R30 million
- **Periods**: Jan/Feb, Mar/Apr, May/Jun, Jul/Aug, Sep/Oct, Nov/Dec
- **Submission**: 25th of month following period

**Quarterly Returns** (Special Permission Required)

- **Requirement**: Annual turnover < R1.5 million
- **Application**: Must apply to SARS for approval
- **Periods**: Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec quarters

### VAT Calculation & Rates

#### Standard Rate: 15%

Applied to most goods and services unless specifically zero-rated or exempt.

#### Zero-Rated Supplies (0% VAT)

**Exports**:

- Physical goods exported from SA
- Services performed outside SA
- International transport services

**Basic Food Items**:

- Brown bread, maize meal, samp
- Milk, eggs, vegetables, fruit
- Rice, lentils, pilchards

**Other Zero-Rated**:

- Petrol and diesel
- Public transport
- Sales of going concerns

#### Exempt Supplies (No VAT)

**Financial Services**:

- Banking services
- Insurance premiums
- Share transactions

**Healthcare**:

- Medical services by qualified practitioners
- Hospital services
- Prescription medicines

**Education**:

- School fees (registered institutions)
- University tuition
- Educational books

**Property**:

- Rental of residential property
- Sale of residential property (by individuals)

#### VAT Calculation Examples

**Example 1: Simple Sale**

```
Sale Price: R1,150 (VAT inclusive)
VAT Amount: R1,150 ÷ 1.15 × 0.15 = R150
Net Sale: R1,150 - R150 = R1,000
```

**Example 2: Purchase with VAT**

```
Purchase: R500 + VAT
VAT Amount: R500 × 0.15 = R75
Total Cost: R500 + R75 = R575
```

### VAT Invoice Requirements

#### Tax Invoice Must Include

1. **Invoice number**: Sequential numbering
2. **Date of issue**: When invoice created
3. **Supplier details**: Name, address, VAT number
4. **Customer details**: Name and address
5. **Description**: Goods/services supplied
6. **Quantity**: Amount of goods/services
7. **Unit price**: Price excluding VAT
8. **VAT amount**: Separately shown
9. **Total amount**: Including VAT

#### Document Retention

- **Period**: 5 years from end of tax year
- **Format**: Original or electronic copies
- **Accessibility**: Available for SARS inspection
- **Backup**: Secure storage recommendations

### Input VAT Claims

#### Qualifying Requirements

- **Tax Invoice**: Proper documentation required
- **Business Purpose**: Must relate to taxable supplies
- **Paid**: VAT must be actually paid to supplier
- **Time Limit**: Claim within 5 years

#### Non-Claimable Input VAT

- **Entertainment**: Business entertainment expenses
- **Private Use**: Personal consumption items
- **Exempt Supplies**: Related to exempt income
- **Capital Goods**: Special rules apply

#### Mixed-Use Assets

**Formula for Partial Claims**:

```
Business Use % × Total VAT = Claimable Amount
```

**Annual Adjustment**:
Review actual business use vs estimated use and adjust accordingly.

## PAYE (Pay As You Earn) Comprehensive Guide

### PAYE System Overview

#### What is PAYE?

PAYE is the system for deducting employees' income tax from their salaries and wages, remitting it to SARS on their behalf.

#### Employer Obligations

- **Registration**: Before first salary payment
- **Deduction**: Calculate and deduct employee tax
- **Payment**: Remit to SARS by 7th of following month
- **Reporting**: Submit monthly EMP201 returns
- **Certificates**: Issue IRP5 certificates annually

### PAYE Registration

#### When to Register

- **First Employee**: Before making first payment
- **Directors**: Company directors are employees
- **Contractors**: Some contractors treated as employees
- **Casual Workers**: Depending on nature of work

#### Required Information

- **Company Details**: Registration numbers, addresses
- **Business Activity**: Nature of business operations
- **Employee Information**: Number and types of employees
- **Remuneration**: Expected salary ranges

#### PAYE Reference Numbers

- **Format**: 7xxxxxxxxx (10 digits starting with 7)
- **Multiple Numbers**: Different business units can have separate numbers
- **Verification**: Available on SARS systems

### PAYE Calculation

#### Gross Remuneration Components

**Regular Payments**:

- Basic salary
- Overtime payments
- Commissions and bonuses
- Allowances (travel, cell phone, etc.)

**Fringe Benefits**:

- Company cars
- Medical aid contributions (employer portion)
- Low-interest loans
- Free or discounted goods/services

#### Deductions from Gross Remuneration

**Retirement Fund Contributions**:

- Pension fund contributions
- Provident fund contributions
- Retirement annuity contributions
- Limit: 27.5% of gross remuneration

**Travel Allowance**:

- Fixed travel allowance: 80% taxable
- Reimbursive travel allowance: Actual business km at R3.98/km
- Company car: Complex calculation based on value and use

#### PAYE Tax Calculation

```
Annual Taxable Income = Gross Remuneration - Allowable Deductions
Annual Tax = Apply Tax Tables (see rates below)
Monthly PAYE = Annual Tax ÷ 12
```

### 2025/26 PAYE Tax Tables

#### Individual Tax Rates

| Annual Taxable Income | Tax Rate |
| --------------------- | -------- |
| R0 - R237,100         | 18%      |
| R237,101 - R370,500   | 26%      |
| R370,501 - R512,800   | 31%      |
| R512,801 - R673,000   | 36%      |
| R673,001 - R857,900   | 39%      |
| R857,901 - R1,817,000 | 41%      |
| R1,817,001 and above  | 45%      |

#### Tax Rebates (Annual)

- **Primary Rebate**: R17,235 (all taxpayers)
- **Secondary Rebate**: R9,444 (65+ years)
- **Tertiary Rebate**: R3,145 (75+ years)

#### Medical Aid Tax Credits (Monthly)

- **Main Member**: R364
- **First Dependent**: R364
- **Additional Dependents**: R246 each

### UIF (Unemployment Insurance Fund)

#### Contribution Rates

- **Employee**: 1% of gross remuneration
- **Employer**: 1% of gross remuneration
- **Total**: 2% of gross remuneration

#### Contribution Limits (2025/26)

- **Monthly Maximum**: R177.12 per person
- **Annual Maximum**: R2,125.44 per person
- **Income Threshold**: Contributions capped at R17,712 monthly earnings

#### UIF Exemptions

- **Foreign Nationals**: On temporary permits
- **Seasonal Workers**: Specific exclusions
- **Learners**: Apprentices and interns
- **Directors**: With >5% shareholding

### SDL (Skills Development Levy)

#### When SDL Applies

- **Payroll Threshold**: Annual payroll > R500,000
- **Rate**: 1% of total payroll
- **Allowances**: 20% training allowance for registered skills programs

#### SDL Calculation

```
Annual Payroll × 1% = SDL Liability
SDL Liability × 20% = Training Allowance (if applicable)
Net SDL = SDL Liability - Training Allowance
```

### Monthly PAYE Compliance

#### EMP201 Return Submission

**Deadline**: 7th of month following pay period
**Content**:

- Total PAYE deducted
- UIF contributions (employee and employer)
- SDL liability (if applicable)
- Employee numbers and details

#### Payment Methods

- **eFiling**: Electronic payment preferred
- **Bank Transfer**: Direct to SARS account
- **Cash**: At SARS branches (not recommended)
- **Debit Order**: Automated monthly payments

#### Late Submission Penalties

- **Administrative Penalty**: R250 per month or part thereof
- **Interest**: 20% per annum on outstanding amounts
- **Additional Penalties**: Up to 200% of tax for serious defaults

### Annual PAYE Compliance

#### IRP5 Certificates

**Issue Date**: By 31 March following tax year
**Recipients**: All employees who received remuneration
**Content**: Complete remuneration and deduction details
**Submission**: Electronic submission to SARS required

#### Reconciliation Process

**Annual Reconciliation Return**: Due 31 May following tax year
**Purpose**: Reconcile monthly EMP201s with annual IRP5s
**Adjustments**: Correct any discrepancies identified

### Employment Tax Incentive (ETI)

#### Qualifying Employees

- **Age**: 18-29 years old
- **Salary**: Maximum R6,500 per month
- **Employment**: Full-time employment
- **Citizenship**: SA citizens, permanent residents, refugees

#### ETI Benefits (Monthly)

**Year 1**:

- Salary R0-R2,000: R1,000 incentive
- Salary R2,001-R4,000: R500 incentive
- Salary R4,001-R6,500: R200 incentive

**Year 2**:

- Reduced rates apply
- Maximum R500 per month

#### ETI Administration

- **Certificates**: ETI certificates required
- **Monthly Claims**: Claim on EMP201 returns
- **Record Keeping**: Maintain employee documentation
- **Compliance**: Regular verification required

### Special PAYE Situations

#### Directors and Shareholders

- **Directors**: Always employees for PAYE purposes
- **Shareholders**: May be employees depending on circumstances
- **Loans**: Fringe benefit implications
- **Dividends**: Separate dividends tax applies

#### Contractors vs Employees

**Employee Indicators**:

- Controlled by employer
- Works exclusively for one entity
- Uses employer's equipment
- Integrated into business

**Contractor Indicators**:

- Control own work methods
- Multiple clients
- Own equipment and tools
- Separate business entity

#### International Employees

- **Tax Residents**: Full PAYE obligations
- **Non-Residents**: PAYE on SA-sourced income
- **Secondments**: Special rules apply
- **Double Tax Treaties**: May affect obligations
